Health Information

Search the most important health conditions in Sierra Leone, with trusted prevention and treatment guidance.

Malaria

High Risk

Malaria is caused by Plasmodium parasites transmitted through bites from infected mosquitoes. It spreads quickly in warm, wet environments where mosquitoes breed.

Common signs include fever, chills, headache, muscle aches, nausea and fatigue. In severe cases, it can cause jaundice, seizures, anemia, and breathing difficulty.

Use insecticide-treated mosquito nets, remove standing water, and apply repellents. Seek early treatment and take prophylactic medicines when traveling to high-risk areas.

Treatment includes ACT (artemisinin-based combination therapy) and medical follow-up. Always consult a qualified health worker early to prevent complications.

Typhoid

Manageable

Typhoid is caused by the Salmonella Typhi bacterium. It spreads through contaminated water and food, especially in areas with poor sanitation.

Symptoms include high fever, headache, weakness, stomach pain, constipation or diarrhea, and sometimes skin rash. It may develop slowly over several days.

Drink boiled or bottled water, practice hand washing, and eat foods that are fully cooked. Vaccination is also available for travelers and people in high-risk areas.

Typhoid is treated with antibiotics and supportive care. Early diagnosis and proper medicine significantly reduce the risk of complications.

Hypertension

Manageable

Hypertension is often caused by a combination of factors such as high salt intake, stress, obesity, and lack of exercise. It may also run in families.

High blood pressure usually has no obvious symptoms. When symptoms appear, they may include headaches, dizziness, or blurred vision.

Reduce salt, eat more fruits and vegetables, exercise regularly, and manage stress. Regular blood pressure checks help catch the condition early.

Treatment may include lifestyle changes and medicines prescribed by a healthcare provider. Routine monitoring and medication adherence are essential.
